SECTION 2 — THE MATHS OF WINNING

"To win consistently we must win four battles every game."

1. 🏀 Win the Rebound Battle

Limit opponent possessions, create additional opportunities; "Box Out. Pursue. Score."

2. 🎯 Get to the Foul Line

Attack the paint, draw fouls, convert free throws.

3. 🔄 Win the Possession Battle

Minimize turnovers, high-quality shot selection.

4. 🤝 Win the Assist Battle

Unselfish play, ball movement, transition passing.

Performance Targets

MetricTarget
Shooting %45% (3-point shooters at 40%+ get the green light)
TurnoversBelow 15 per game — BALL SECURITY
ReboundingOver 55% per game — 'BOX' & 'GLASS'
Free Throw %Over 70% (minimum 20 attempts) — 'attack the paint'
Assists20+ per game — 'PUSH' and 'EXTRA'
Possession Share50%+ per game — WIN the ball battle

⚠️ Dead Over Live

In pressure situations, a dead-ball turnover is a team success over a live-ball turnover that gifts transition points.

An 8-second call, a 5-second inbound count, or a held ball that stops the break is often the right play.

What we avoid: the soft pass picked off, the loose handle in traffic, the blind advance pass that becomes a run-out layup.

"Protect home. Dead over live."

SECTION 3 — OFFENSIVE SYSTEM

3.1 Offensive Identity

SEEK

Scan for mismatches, poor defensive positioning, spacing breakdowns. Eyes and IQ are our weapons.

CREATE

When advantages don't exist, manufacture them through spacing, movement, screens, cuts, ball movement.

EXPLOIT

Once identified, attack decisively without hesitation. Indecision kills advantages.

3.2 Offensive Objectives

  • Great Spacing — spacing is king
  • Make two guard one — hand-offs, drives, exchanges, screens (on and off ball)
  • Win close-outs — attack close-outs (penetrate or pass)
  • Seek Mismatches — create and hunt mismatches

3.3 The 4-Out 1-In Motion Offense

Benefits: Creates Optimal Spacing, Develops Basketball IQ, Exploits Mismatches, Emphasizes Movement and Pace, Difficult to Scout, Utilises the Post Player.

Post Player Rules

  • ALWAYS start on weak side (opposite ball)
  • Flash to strong side ONLY for sealing opportunities or specific actions
  • Duck-in for 2-count on ball reversals
  • Pin defenders on corner passes

3.4 Fundamental Reads

1. SLOT TO WING — "THE WAVE"

Trigger: Slot-to-Wing pass

Action: Passer immediately basket cuts — ALWAYS CUT TO SCORE

2. BALL REVERSAL / DOUBLE

Trigger: Ball reverses slot-to-slot

Action: Weak side players exchange positions

3. WING TO POST — SPLIT

Trigger: Wing-to-Post pass

Action: Passer screens away for nearest player

4. BALL SCREENS

Types: UP  /  DOWN  /  FIST  /  TWIX

Call "RED" — late shot clock signal (<6 sec) triggering an immediate ball screen for the called player.

Ball handler reads: USE / IGNORE / SPLIT / SNAKE the screen based on defensive alignment.

Perimeter Rules

  • Always fill the spot closest to the ball
  • Fill to open corners once inside 3-point line
  • After passing: BASKET CUT or SCREEN AWAY — always one or the other
  • If ball is dribbled AT you: BACK CUT immediately
  • Move after every pass — no standing still

3.5 Screening Concepts

OFF-BALL SCREENS: Slot-to-Slot Away Screen, Post Player Away Screens. Options: CURL / STRAIGHT CUT / BACK CUT / FLARE.

ON-BALL SCREENS: Slot Lift Screen, Wing Screen. Reads: USE / IGNORE / SPLIT / SNAKE.

3.7 Transition Offence — The PUSH

"Win the rebound — win the race." The PUSH is our transition system and the engine behind the Rebound & Run principle. Every player must know their role from the moment the ball is secured.

  • Secure the Rebound — GLASS
    Crash the glass with intent; secure possession before thinking about transition.
  • Outlet — Butt to sideline, then PUSH
    Rebounder pivots away from the defence — butt to the sideline — and fires the outlet pass to push the ball into the middle.
  • Fill the Lanes — sprint wide
    Non-rebounders immediately sprint to fill the wide lanes. One player on each side of the floor, stretching the defence.
  • Early Pass — pass ahead
    Push the ball ahead whenever a scoring advantage exists. Don't dribble what you can pass.
  • Trailers fill the Slot Spots
    The two trailing players fill the slot positions to provide floor balance and secondary scoring options.
  • DRAG Pick & Roll — SLOT to SLOT
    If the defence stops the initial advantage, the trailing ballhandler calls the DRAG screen. Slot-to-slot ball screen — read the coverage: USE / IGNORE / SPLIT / SNAKE.

3.8 Special Situations — Set Plays

The following set plays are used in specific game situations. Know your role in each action and be ready to execute on the coach's call.

🏀 SIDE — Sideline Out of Bounds

Sideline out-of-bounds. Attack quickly from the wing or corner — initiate action before the defence can set.

🏀 DOUBLE — Baseline Out of Bounds

Double screen action. Two coordinated screens release a shooter or cutter for a high-quality, uncontested look.

🏀 BOX — Baseline Out of Bounds

Box formation inbound. Use to free up shooters and cutters from a box set — split the defence with coordinated movement.

🏀 HEAD TAP — After Time Out (ATO)

ATO set for a clean shot. Use when you need a high-percentage look coming out of a timeout. Execute with pace and precision.

"Nothing Easy — Anticipate. Disrupt. Defend."

4.2 Seven Defensive Non-Negotiables

  • STANCE at all times — point of attack, once away, help-side
  • Talk and echo every trip — matchups, screens, tags, calls
  • Contest the outlet — clean the glass below the foul line
  • No defending in dead space — no gambling between the lines
  • Contain first — pressure with feet; no reaching, no gambling
  • Talk screens early — NEVER go under on shooters
  • Box out before rebounding — finish every possession

4.3 Off-Ball Gravity

Success is not only touching the ball. A hard cut, deliberate lift, strong screen, or deep seal that draws two defenders is a winning play for this programme, even if you never receive the pass.

We value:

  • Drawing help and shifting the low man
  • Opening driving lanes through purposeful movement
  • Creating the extra pass through pressure and movement
"Your movement creates value, even when the stat sheet doesn't show it."

7.2 Game Day Standards

Timeout Protocol

Timeouts are 40 seconds. They are for coaching, not reacting.

Structure:

  1. What is happening?
  2. What are we changing?
  3. What is the first action out of the timeout?

Coaches give the key message. Players listen, lock in, clarify only what matters. One tactical focus. One behavioural focus. One reminder.

On-Court Huddles

At every meaningful stoppage, we get together quickly, make eye contact, and communicate calmly.

Standard phrases:

  • "What's next?"
  • "Same standards"
  • "Protect home"
  • "Next stop, next score"

Huddles build trust, calm emotion, and sharpen our next action. They are part of our tactical model — not just our personality.
